Why we built a different room

The Insight! Report tells you the truth in numbers. The Genius! Editor asks you the questions numbers cannot. Both belong on your desk — but during essay season, only one of them will actually help you write the first sentence.

The Insight! Report

Data-driven. Realistic. Comparative. Built on 50,000+ admissions, it tells you where you stand.

  • GPA, scores, activities, school list
  • Reach / Target / Safety prediction
  • What to fix first

The Genius! Editor

Imagination-driven. Bold. Personal. Forty-four questions designed to startle out the sentence only you can write.

  • Memory, contradiction, fear, obsession
  • Bold dreams and impossible ambitions
  • A spark sheet for Common App + every supplement

Who walks out of here with their essay started

Most students sit down to draft the Common App and freeze. The questions here aren't trying to be cute — they are designed to walk past your prepared answers and land on the things you actually think about.

For the blank-page student

You have lived a life. You just need someone to ask the right question. Forty-four of them, in nine themed rooms, pulled from a decade of essay coaching.

For the polished student

Your draft sounds careful. Too careful. These prompts pry the polite first answer off and ask what you'd say at 4 a.m. instead.

For the supplemental writer

Why X? Why this major? Community? Activity? The school-specific room sharpens the answers that make a "Why X" stop sounding like every other "Why X."

How the Editor works

1

Walk through nine rooms

Memory, identity, what lights you up, the dark room, the people you carry, wild questions, bold dreams, school-specific lenses, and finally — voice. Each room has four to five prompts.

2

Write fragments, not paragraphs

Two sentences. A list. A single image. The Editor saves locally as you type — leave anything blank, skip a room, come back tomorrow.

3

Read your spark sheet

At the end, every answer you wrote is bundled into one printable sheet, grouped by room. This is the page you bring to your draft — or to your counselor.

4

Map the sparks to prompts

The spark sheet ends with a short guide on which fragments tend to feed which essay prompt — Common App #1 through #7 and the most common supplements.

A few notes before you start

  • Don't try to be impressive. Try to be specific.
  • If a prompt makes you flinch, that one is probably the one to write.
  • It is fine to skip rooms. The spark sheet still works on what you wrote.
  • Use the language you think in — Korean, English, or both. Translate later.
